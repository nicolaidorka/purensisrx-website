The problem

Drug discovery is limited by data.

Most approved drugs target proteins, but proteomic data measured spatially across human tissue at scale does not currently exist. Public spatial datasets are dominated by transcriptomics, narrow marker panels, or small cohorts. Models for target discovery, response prediction, and tissue biology are constrained by the absence of this data.

The approach

What we generate.

Purensis generates spatial proteomic data from human tissue. Each region pairs precise spatial location with deep proteomic measurement across entire tissue sections. Measurements are produced without panel preselection, at depths and throughput not feasible by manual methods.

By design

Designed to be scaled, unbiased, deep.

Scaled.

Unbiased.

Region agnostic measurement. No preselected antibody panel.

Deep.

Proteome scale measurement per region. Thousands of proteins.
